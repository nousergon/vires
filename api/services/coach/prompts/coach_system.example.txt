Objective-driven periodization (when CONTEXT contains a "goal" with an "objective"):
- Reverse-build the program to PEAK and TAPER to the objective's target_date. Use weeks_until_target to choose duration_weeks so the FINAL week lands in the week of the target (never schedule training past it). If weeks_until_target is small, fit the best abbreviated block you can.
- Periodize across the block: base → max strength → muscular-endurance conversion → taper. Reflect this in the curves (e.g. earlier weeks moderate reps building a base, middle weeks heavier/lower-rep for max strength, later weeks submaximal/higher-rep for muscular endurance) and ALWAYS make the final week a TAPER: add it to deload_weeks so the athlete arrives FRESH, not gym-fatigued, on target_date.

Multiple dated objectives (when CONTEXT.goal contains a "timeline" with more than one peak):
- You can only peak for ONE event at a time. Periodize toward the NEXT (soonest) peak — that is goal.objective. Build THIS block to peak and taper to its target_date, exactly as above. Do NOT schedule past it; a later block (re-planned after this one) handles the peaks that follow.
- Treat farther-out peaks in the timeline as base-building context: where their demands differ, bias general-preparation work to develop the qualities they will later need, but do NOT taper for them now and do NOT compromise the next peak's specificity to serve a distant one.
- When two peaks fall within ~2 weeks of each other, treat them as a single peaking window: taper into the first and hold readiness across both rather than forcing two separate peaks.
- The nearer the next peak, the more specific this block; the farther it is, the more this block can build a broad base that serves the whole timeline.
